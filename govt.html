1.Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)

SNAP, commonly known as food stamps, is a federal program that provides low-income individuals and families with funds to purchase groceries. The program issues benefits through an Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) card, which can be used at most grocery stores and some farmers' markets. SNAP aims to improve food security and promote access to nutritious foods. Eligibility is based on income, household size, and other factors.

2.Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) Program

The WIC program supports low-income pregnant women, new mothers, and children under the age of five by providing healthy food, nutrition education, and breastfeeding support. Participants receive vouchers or an EBT card to purchase nutritious foods such as milk, eggs, fruits, and vegetables. The program also offers healthcare referrals and nutrition counseling to ensure proper child development and maternal health.

3.National School Lunch Program (NSLP) & School Breakfast Program (SBP)

These federally assisted meal programs provide free or reduced-price meals to children in public and nonprofit private schools. The goal is to ensure that children receive balanced meals that support their academic performance and overall health. The programs follow federal nutrition guidelines to provide wholesome meals, including fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and protein sources.

4.The Emergency Food Assistance Program (TEFAP)

TEFAP is a federal program that supplies food banks and pantries with free, nutritious food for low-income families. The program distributes USDA-purchased food to local agencies, which then provide food assistance to households in need. TEFAP helps to supplement the diets of individuals facing food insecurity by providing essential staples such as canned goods, frozen meats, and dairy products.

5.Meals on Wheels

Meals on Wheels is a community-based program that delivers nutritious meals to seniors who are homebound or have difficulty preparing their own food. The program not only provides food but also offers regular wellness checks and social interaction to reduce isolation among the elderly. Funded through government grants and community donations, Meals on Wheels helps seniors maintain independence while ensuring they receive adequate nutrition.

6.Summer Food Service Program (SFSP)

SFSP provides free meals to children in low-income areas during the summer months when school is not in session. This program ensures that children who rely on school meals during the academic year continue to receive nutritious food. Local schools, nonprofit organizations, and community centers serve as meal distribution sites to help prevent child hunger during summer break.

7.Commodity Supplemental Food Program (CSFP)

CSFP is designed to improve the health of low-income seniors aged 60 and older by providing them with nutritious food packages. These food packages include a variety of staple foods such as canned fruits and vegetables, dairy products, grains, and proteins. The program aims to reduce food insecurity and support the dietary needs of elderly individuals who may struggle with access to healthy food.

8.Poshan Abhiyaan (National Nutrition Mission)

Launched by the Government of India in 2018, this mission aims to tackle malnutrition and improve nutritional outcomes for children, adolescent girls, pregnant women, and lactating mothers. It focuses on promoting behavior change and leveraging technology to monitor progress. The initiative includes awareness campaigns about proper diet, hygiene, and health practices to reduce stunting, undernutrition, anemia, and low birth weights.
